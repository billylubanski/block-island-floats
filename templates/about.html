How the hunt works

A few rules shape the whole season

The Glass Float Project started in 2011 and still follows a few dependable patterns that matter when you plan a day on the island.

Floats arrive in waves

They are hidden throughout the season instead of all at once, so the hunt keeps changing as the summer moves.

Most hides stay on public routes

Beaches and marked Greenway trails do most of the work, not sensitive areas, dunes, or private property.

One float per person

The project runs on stewardship. Find one, register it, and leave the next discovery for someone else.

Registering keeps the story intact

The official archive ties each float to a finder photo and story, which is why the registration link matters even if you planned the hunt here.
